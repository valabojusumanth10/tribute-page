A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man Of India

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, often referred to as the "People's President" and the "Missile Man of India," served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he was a renowned scientist and engineer who played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development. Kalam's leadership and vision significantly contributed to the successful development of India's first satellite launch vehicle and the Pokhran-II nuclear tests. His deep commitment to education led to the popularization of science and technology among the youth. Known for his humility, simplicity, and dedication to the country, Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam left an indelible mark on India's scientific, political, and educational landscape. His inspirational speeches and writings continue to motivate people, especially the youth, to pursue their dreams and contribute to the progress of the nation.

Biography

  • Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. Came from a modest background, faced financial challenges in his early life.
  • Graduated in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology in 1958. Joined the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) as a scientist.
  • Joined ISRO in 1969, contributing significantly to India's space capabilities. Led the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP), resulting in the development of key missiles.
